Vampire Amigurumi Pattern



Meet this snuggly crocheted vampire! A fun, spooky companion for the Halloween! Don't worry, he don't bite.p>

Crochet it today with our step-by-step Fox Pattern!



Materials:
  • 4 ply yarn
  • 2 mm crochet hook
  • 8 mm safety eyes
  • Fiberfill
  • Black embroidery floss
  • Sewing needle and scissors
Abbreviations:

ch = chain

st = stitch

sc = single crochet

inc = increase (2 sc in 1 st)

dec = decrease (2 sc together)

sl st = slip stitch

dc = double crochet

tr = treble crochet

(..) = repeat the instructions in brackets based on the given no. of times

[..] = total number of stitches



Head:

Crochet with orange yarn.

Rnd 1: sc x6 in magic ring [6]

Rnd 2: inc x6 [12]

Rnd 3: (sc, inc) x6 [18]

Rnd 4: (sc x2 inc) x6 [24]

Rnd 5: (sc x3, inc) x6 [30]

Rnd 6: (sc x4, inc) x6 [36]

Rnd 7: (sc x5, inc) x6 [42]

Rnd 8-13: sc x42 [42]



Change to white yarn.

Rnd 14: (sc x5, dec) x6 [36]



Insert safety eyes between Rnd 12 and Rnd 13 (6 st in between the eyes).

Rnd 15: (sc x4, dec) x6 [30]

Rnd 16: (sc x3, dec) x6 [24]

Rnd 17: (sc x2, dec) x6 [18]

Stuff with fiberfill.



Rnd 18: (sc, dec) x6 [12]

Stuff with fiberfill.



Body (make 2):

Change to orange yarn

Rnd 19: sc x12 [12]

Rnd 20: (sc, inc) x6 [18]

Rnd 21: sc x180 [18]

Rnd 22: (sc x2, inc) x6 [24]

Rnd 23: sc x24 [24]

Rnd 24: (sc x11, inc) x2 [26]

Rnd 25-29: sc x26 [26]



Legs:

Rnd 1: crochet the 1st rnd of the leg: make sc in each st until the marker then turn and crochet on the other side of the body, forming a circle [13]

Rnd 2-5: sc x13 [13]

Rnd 4: dec x6 [7]

Fasten off leaving a small tail.



Join the yarn between the legs and crochet the second leg on the 13 st of the body in the same way.



Arms (make 2):

Work with black yarn.

Rnd 1: sc x5 in magic ring [5]

Rnd 2: inc x5 [10]

Rnd 3-5: sc x10 [10]

Stuff the bottom of the arm with fiberfill.



Change to orange yarn.

Rnd 6: (sc, dec) x3, sc [7]

Rnd 7-11: sc x7 [7]

Flatten the edges and crochet 3 sc through both layers, closing the hole.



Muzzle:

Change to white yarn.

Rnd 1: sc x6 in magic ring [6]

Rnd 2: (sc, inc) x3 [9]

Rnd 3: (sc x2, inc) x3 [12]

Rnd 4: sc x12 [12]

Finish with sl st. Fasten off and stuff a little bit.



Ears:

Start with black yarn.

Rnd 1: sc x4 in magic ring [4]



Change to orange yarn.

Rnd 2: inc x4 [8]

Rnd 3: (sc x3, inc) x2 [10]

Rnd 4: (sc x4, inc) x2 [12]

Rnd 5: (sc x5, inc) x2 [14]

Finish with sl st. Fasten off.



Tail:

Begin with white yarn.

Rnd 1: sc x4 in magic ring [4]

Rnd 2: inc, sc x3 [4]

Rnd 3: inc x3, sc x2 [8]



Change to orange yarn.

Rnd 4: (sc, inc) x4 [12

Rnd 5-7: sc x12 x4 [12]

Rnd 8: (sc, dec) x4 [8]

Rnd 9: (sc x2, dec) x2 [6]

Rnd 10: sc x6 [6]

Finish with sl st. Fasten off.



Belly:

Begin with white yarn.

Rnd 1: sc x6 in magic ring [6]

Rnd 2: inc x6 [12]

Turn and crochet in opposite direction.



Rnd 3: ch 1, skip 1 st, sc, 3 hdc, hdc, sc x2, 3 sc, sc x2, hdc, 3 hdc, sc, sl st [12]

Fasten off.



Bow:

Begin with link pink yarn.

Rnd 1: dc x8 in magic ring [8]

Wrap the middle of the bow with yarn end a couple of times and tie a knot.



Assembly:
  1. Sew on the muzzle and embroider a nose.
  2. Embroider lashes.
  3. Pin the ears to the head at Rnds 4-8, then sew them on.
  4. Sew on the arms at Rnd 19 of the body.
  5. Sew on the belly in between Rnd 22 and Rnd 29
  6. Sew on the tail at Rnds 27-28.
  7. Sew on the bow near the ear.
  8. Tint the cheeks with rouge.


Great job! Your vampire amigurumi is done! Happy Halloween! Share your finished work and tag us in Instagram!
